## How to Choose a Drone Inspection Service in New Orleans

- **Verify FAA Part 107 certification first, full stop.** Any commercial drone flight requires it. Ask for the certificate number and cross-check it at FAA DroneZone. In a market flooded with post-Ida "drone inspectors," this single step filters out roughly half the field.
- **Confirm they carry commercial drone liability insurance — minimum $1M per occurrence.** Homeowner's and general liability policies don't cover UAV operations. Ask for a certificate of insurance naming your project as an additionally insured party before any deposit changes hands.
- **Look for thermal imaging credentials on industrial or roof work.** RGB photos show surface damage. Thermal shows what's happening underneath — moisture intrusion, insulation gaps, electrical hot spots. ITC Level I or ASNT Level II certifications mean the pilot can actually interpret the data, not just capture it.
- **Ask specifically about Louisiana coastal airspace familiarity.** New Orleans sits in Class C airspace around Louis Armstrong International, with TFRs that drop regularly over the Superdome and the port. A pilot who's never navigated LAANSS authorizations here will cost you time and money mid-project.
- **For post-storm insurance work, ask about FEMA IS-1505 UAS Disaster Operations training.** After a named storm, the FAA establishes TFRs over affected parishes within hours. Operators with disaster-operations training know how to coordinate with local emergency management for access before the TFR lifts.

> **Pro Tip:** New Orleans's industrial corridor — the stretch of refineries, chemical plants, and grain elevators running from Norco to Chalmette — often requires operators to have OSHA 10-Hour Construction Safety certification and facility-specific badging just to get on site. If you're contracting for industrial inspection work along the river, ask whether the operator has worked in that corridor before. A 45-minute badge process on day one is a lot cheaper than discovering it mid-mobilization.

## What to Expect

Drone inspection services in New Orleans run $500–$3,500 per inspection depending on scope: a single-family roof assessment sits at the low end, while a multi-structure industrial facility survey with full thermal overlay and measurement report lands at the top. Most operators deliver a geotagged photo set and preliminary findings within 24 hours; full reports with thermal anomaly analysis typically take 48 hours.

> **Reality Check:** "Drone inspection" pricing varies wildly because operators often quote the flight separately from the deliverable. A $350 quote that doesn't include data processing, report generation, or a revisit if weather scrubs the first attempt will end up costing you $700. Get an all-in price for the final report, not just the flight.

New Orleans sits in a uniquely demanding environment for aerial inspection work — the combination of subtropical humidity, hurricane-season building damage cycles, and an aging infrastructure corridor along the Mississippi River means demand for qualified operators spikes hard after any named storm and stays elevated for months. The city's position as a major port and petrochemical hub also means a disproportionate share of the commercial drone work here is industrial-grade, with operators who handle routine utility and refinery surveys year-round rather than treating inspection as a side gig to real estate photography. That's the market to fish in — the operators who are busy in May are the ones you want after the next August storm.
