## How to Choose a Drone Inspection Service in Chicago

- **Verify Part 107 certification before anything else.** Ask for the FAA certificate number and cross-check it at faa.gov/licenses_certificates. Chicago falls under Class B airspace controlled by O'Hare and Midway approach corridors — any legitimate operator will already have LAANC authorizations or COAs in place for urban flight. If they don't know what LAANC is, hang up.
- **Match the sensor payload to the job.** A roof assessment after a hail event needs high-resolution RGB, but a ComEd substation inspection or flat-roof membrane survey needs thermal (ITC Level I or ASNT Level II certified pilot). Asking "what cameras do you carry" separates generalists from specialists fast.
- **Ask for a Chicago-specific portfolio.** Inspecting a 40-story mixed-use tower on the Near North Side in 25 mph lake winds is not the same as inspecting a warehouse roof in Romeoville. Look for evidence they've worked on Chicago's building stock — masonry facades, flat ballasted roofs, the kind of infrastructure that lines the industrial corridor along the Chicago River.
- **Confirm insurance coverage, not just the drone operator's policy.** You want to see a commercial general liability policy (minimum $1M) that covers the work product, not just the aircraft hull. General contractors in particular should require being named as an additional insured.
- **Request a sample deliverable.** A real operator delivers geotagged photo sets, annotated thermal overlays, and a written summary — typically in 24-48 hours. If the "report" is a Google Drive folder of unlabeled JPEGs, that's not a deliverable, that's homework.

> **Pro Tip:** For post-storm insurance documentation in Cook County, timing matters more than almost anything else. The best operators in Chicago keep slots open within 24 hours of a major hail or wind event specifically for insurance adjusters. Ask whether they have a rapid-response protocol — if they do, they've probably already done this dozens of times.

## What to Expect

Drone inspections in Chicago run $500–3,500 per engagement depending on scope: a single-family roof assessment sits at the low end, while a full transmission line survey or multi-structure industrial facility inspection lands at the top. Most straightforward commercial jobs — a 10,000 sq ft flat roof, a cell tower, a bridge deck — fall in the $800–1,800 range with a 24–48 hour turnaround on deliverables. The operator conducts a site survey, files any required airspace authorizations, executes the flight, and processes imagery before sending a structured report.

> **Reality Check:** The cheapest quote almost always means the pilot skipped payload — they're flying RGB-only and calling it a "thermal inspection." For anything involving moisture intrusion, HVAC, or electrical infrastructure, thermal imaging is non-negotiable, and a certified thermographer costs more for a reason. A $400 inspection that misses a failing roof membrane costs you $40,000 in water damage two winters later.
