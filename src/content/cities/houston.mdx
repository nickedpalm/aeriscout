## How to Choose a Drone Inspection Service in Houston

- **Verify the FAA Part 107 certificate before anything else.** Texas has no additional state licensing layer, which means the federal certificate is the only credential that matters legally. Ask for the certificate number and cross-check it at [faa.gov/uas/recreational_fliers/knowledge_test_updates](https://faa.gov/uas/recreational_fliers/knowledge_test_updates). If they can't produce it in 30 seconds, walk away.
- **Match the camera payload to the job.** A roof assessment after a hail storm needs thermal imaging — not just RGB photography. Houston's humidity and heat create significant thermal contrast, which makes ITC-certified thermographers genuinely more useful here than in drier markets. Ask specifically what sensors are onboard.
- **Check their airspace authorization track record.** Houston sits under multiple Class B and Class C airspace corridors from IAH and HOU. Any operator who doesn't immediately mention LAANC authorization for jobs near those corridors has either never worked there or is cutting corners.
- **Ask for a sample deliverable, not just a portfolio.** Photos are easy to cherry-pick. Ask for a sample report — geotagged imagery, thermal overlays, measurement data, the works. A professional outfit delivering within 24-48 hours should have no problem showing you what that looks like in practice.
- **Insurance is non-negotiable for commercial work.** General contractors and utilities: your contracts almost certainly require hull and liability coverage. A minimum of $1M general liability is standard. Ask for the certificate of insurance, not their word for it.

> **Pro Tip:** Houston's petrochemical corridor along the Ship Channel is some of the most complex airspace and regulatory territory for drone operators in the country. If you're hiring for industrial facility inspections in that zone, specifically ask whether they've worked with BSEE or EPA compliance documentation requirements. It's a different world from a rooftop assessment.

## What to Expect

Most commercial drone inspections in Houston run $500–$3,500 depending on scope — a straightforward residential roof assessment sits at the low end, while a multi-tower utility survey or large-scale solar array inspection with full thermal reporting pushes toward the top. Turnaround on deliverables is typically 24–48 hours after the flight, though complex thermal analysis or large commercial sites can stretch to 72 hours.

> **Reality Check:** The biggest pricing mistake buyers make is hiring on day rate without specifying deliverables. A four-hour flight that produces 2,000 unorganized JPEG files is not the same as a flight that produces a structured inspection report with annotated anomalies and measurement data. Get the deliverable format locked in writing before the bird goes up, or you'll pay a second time to get the data into usable shape.

## Local Market Overview

Houston's construction and energy sectors drive some of the highest demand for drone inspection services in the country — the metro area regularly tops national rankings for both commercial construction starts and industrial facility density, and post-hurricane insurance claims (Harvey alone triggered over $125 billion in damages) have made rapid aerial loss documentation a standard line item for adjusters operating here. The Texas Department of Insurance doesn't mandate drone-based inspections, but carriers have quietly made them the default for large commercial claims, which means demand in the Houston market is structural, not cyclical.
