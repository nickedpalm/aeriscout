## How to Choose a Drone Inspection Service in Wichita

- **Verify Part 107 before anything else.** In Kansas, operating a commercial UAV without an FAA Remote Pilot Certificate isn't a gray area — it's a federal violation, and any data collected could be challenged in an insurance claim or legal proceeding. Ask for the certificate number and cross-reference it at the FAA's DroneZone registry.
- **Match the sensor package to your job.** Wichita's hailstorm season (peak: May–July) generates a massive rush of roof insurance claims. If you're a carrier or adjuster, you need a pilot who can run thermal alongside RGB — visual-only imagery misses granule loss and moisture intrusion that shows up clearly on a FLIR sensor.
- **Ask specifically about Wichita airspace.** Mid-Continent Airport (ICT) sits in Class C airspace, and McConnell Air Force Base creates a hard-floor restricted zone to the southeast. A competent local pilot will have pre-authorized LAANC waivers ready; an out-of-towner might not even know to ask.
- **Check for vertical-specific credentials.** Cell tower surveys require comfort working near energized infrastructure. Solar array inspections call for ITC Level I Thermography at minimum. Roof assessments tied to insurance claims benefit from pilots who carry OSHA 10-Hour cards — some carriers require it before they'll accept the report.
- **Get a sample deliverable before you commit.** A professional inspection doesn't end with a Dropbox link of JPEGs. Ask to see a completed report: geotagged photo sets, thermal overlays with anomaly callouts, and measurement data in a format your team can actually use.

> **Pro Tip:** Wichita contractors working on projects near the Arkansas River corridor or Keeper of the Plains should confirm their pilot has experience with low-altitude flight near structures and water — wind behavior is genuinely different there, and it shows in footage quality.

## What to Expect

Inspection pricing in Wichita runs $500–3,500 depending on asset type and scope: a residential roof assessment for an insurance claim sits at the low end, while a full cell tower inspection with structural annotation or a multi-acre solar farm thermal sweep lands toward the top. Most pilots deliver geotagged photo packages and a formal PDF report within 24–48 hours of flight.

> **Reality Check:** The biggest pricing mistake buyers make is comparing a $350 "drone video" quote to a $1,200 inspection quote. They're not the same service. The cheap quote gets you footage. The inspection quote gets you a defensible report with GPS coordinates, anomaly markings, and measurement data — the kind of document that holds up when an insurer or engineer reviews it.
