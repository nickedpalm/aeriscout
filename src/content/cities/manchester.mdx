Finding a drone inspection service in [Manchester](/locations/new-hampshire/manchester/), New Hampshire shouldn't feel like guessing — but it often does. ## How to Choose a Drone Inspection Service in Manchester

- **Verify FAA Part 107 certification before anything else.** New Hampshire doesn't add a state-level UAS license on top of federal requirements, so Part 107 is the floor — not a bonus. Ask for the certificate number and confirm it on the FAA's DroneZone registry. Anyone who fumbles this question is a hobbyist with a DJI.
- **Match the sensor package to your job.** A roof inspection after a hailstorm needs RGB for visible damage and thermal for moisture intrusion. Transmission line surveys need multispectral or high-zoom optical. Ask specifically what camera payload the pilot is flying — "we have a drone" is not an answer.
- **Check for OSHA 10 and site safety documentation.** Manchester's construction corridor along Elm Street and the I-293 industrial belt means most commercial sites require documented safety compliance before a vendor sets foot on the property. Pilots who don't carry OSHA 10 will stall your job.
- **Ask about airspace authorizations.** Manchester-[Boston](/locations/massachusetts/boston/) Regional Airport (MHT) creates Class C airspace that covers significant parts of the city. Legitimate operators pull LAANC authorizations automatically — if they're surprised by the question, they've been flying illegally.
- **Request a sample deliverable, not just a portfolio.** A polished Instagram reel means nothing. Ask for a sample inspection report — geotagged photo sets, thermal overlays, measurement annotations. That's what you'll actually hand to an engineer or adjuster.

> **Pro Tip:** For insurance loss documentation specifically, confirm the pilot has worked directly with adjusters before. The deliverable format matters — some carriers require specific metadata fields and coordinate systems that a technically competent but adjuster-naive pilot will get wrong on the first pass.

## What to Expect

Drone inspections in the Manchester market run $500–$3,500 per inspection depending on asset type, sensor package, and deliverable complexity — a basic residential roof assessment sits at the low end, while a multi-structure industrial facility survey with thermal analysis and a formal report pushes toward the ceiling. Most operators turn around deliverables within 24–48 hours of the flight, though complex thermal analysis reports may require an additional day for annotation.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake is hiring on day rate rather than deliverable scope. A pilot who quotes $300/day may take three days and deliver raw footage. A pilot who quotes $1,200 flat delivers a formatted report your engineer can act on immediately. The cheaper number is often the more expensive outcome.
