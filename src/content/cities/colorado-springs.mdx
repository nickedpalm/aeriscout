## How to Choose a Drone Inspection Service in Colorado Springs

- **Verify the Part 107 certificate before anything else.** FAA certification is the legal baseline for commercial drone operations — not a differentiator, a minimum. Ask for the certificate number and cross-check it at the FAA DroniTect Registry. In Colorado, where Front Range wind events can ground even experienced pilots, you also want to ask how they handle rescheduling when conditions deteriorate at altitude.
- **Match the sensor payload to your inspection type.** RGB cameras catch visible damage on roof membranes and fascia; thermal (FLIR or similar) is non-negotiable for solar array hotspot detection, flat roof moisture mapping, and electrical infrastructure work. If you're commissioning a cell tower or transmission line survey, ask specifically whether they have a zoom payload — wide-angle lenses miss what you're paying to find.
- **Check insurance coverage, not just existence.** Colorado Springs contractors and utilities should require a minimum of $1M hull and liability coverage per flight. Ask for the certificate of insurance naming your company as additional insured. A pilot who hesitates on this is telling you something.
- **Ask about deliverable format before you book.** A high-res geotagged photo set is table stakes. For infrastructure work, you want orthomosaic maps, thermal overlays with temperature delta annotations, and a written anomaly report — not just a Dropbox link to 400 raw JPEGs. Get the deliverable spec in writing before deposit.
- **Look for Colorado-specific experience.** Pikes Peak region terrain creates orographic lift and afternoon convective activity that eats inexperienced pilots for lunch. Pilots who've worked the I-25 corridor utility corridors or post-hail residential runs along the Briargate and Falcon areas know how to adapt flight plans to real conditions here.

> **Pro Tip:** Colorado Springs sits in a Class E airspace environment with proximity to Peterson Space Force Base, Schriever SFB, and Fort Carson. Any commercial pilot working in this market should be fluent in LAANC authorization workflows and know which zones require manual waivers. If they don't mention airspace when you ask about logistics, that's a gap.

## What to Expect

Drone inspection jobs in Colorado Springs typically run $500–$3,500 depending on structure type, sensor payload, and deliverable complexity — a basic residential roof assessment lands near the low end, while a full industrial facility inspection with thermal mapping and a written engineering-style report sits at the top. Most reputable operators turn around deliverables within 24–48 hours for standard inspections, with same-day rush options available for insurance loss documentation after hail events (and the Front Range gets plenty of those).

> **Reality Check:** The biggest pricing mistake buyers make is comparing a flat-rate quote that includes thermal imagery and a written report against a cheaper quote that's just flight time and raw photos. They are not the same product. Always ask what the deliverable package includes before treating two quotes as comparable numbers.
