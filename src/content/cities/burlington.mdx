## How to Choose a Drone Inspection Service in Burlington

- **Verify FAA Part 107 certification before anything else.** Vermont has no state-level UAS licensing layer, so federal Part 107 is the floor. Ask for the certificate number and cross-check it at the FAA DroniS database. If a pilot can't produce it in 30 seconds, move on.
- **Match the sensor to the job.** RGB cameras work for construction progress and roof documentation. Thermal sensors (look for ITC Level I or ASNT Level II credentials) are non-negotiable for solar array inspections and HVAC diagnostics. Don't pay thermal rates for an RGB shoot — and don't accept RGB results when you needed thermal.
- **Check their Burlington airspace experience.** Burlington International Airport sits 3 miles southeast of downtown, placing much of the city under Class C airspace. Pilots need prior authorization via LAANC for many commercial jobs here. Ask if they've flown in Class C before — the answer tells you a lot.
- **Ask for a sample deliverable, not just sample photos.** A professional inspection report includes geotagged imagery, a thermal overlay where applicable, measurement annotations, and a written summary. A folder of JPEGs is not a report.
- **Confirm they carry commercial UAS insurance.** Vermont's construction and utility sectors increasingly require $1M+ liability coverage on drone contractors. A homeowner policy doesn't cover commercial operations.

> **Pro Tip:** Burlington's weather is genuinely brutal from November through March — wind, ice fog, and rapid temperature swings all degrade thermal data quality. If you're scheduling a thermal inspection, push for a window between April and October, ideally on a calm morning after overnight temperatures dropped at least 15°F to maximize thermal contrast.

## What to Expect

Commercial drone inspections in Burlington run $500–$3,500 depending on site complexity, flight time, sensor type, and report depth — a straightforward residential roof assessment lands near the low end; a transmission line survey or multi-building industrial facility lands near the top. Most operators deliver geotagged photo sets and a written report within 24–48 hours of the flight, with thermal overlays sometimes requiring an extra day for processing.

> **Reality Check:** The biggest pricing mistake buyers make is comparing quotes without specifying deliverables. A $400 quote and a $1,200 quote may both say "roof inspection," but one includes a 20-page annotated PDF and one includes a Google Drive link. Get the deliverable format in writing before you sign anything.
