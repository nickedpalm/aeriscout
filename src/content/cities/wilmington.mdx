## How to Choose a Drone Inspection Service in Wilmington

- **Verify Part 107 before anything else.** Delaware enforces FAA regulations the same as anywhere, but the Wilmington Airport (ILG) sits close enough to the city that many jobs require airspace authorization through LAANC. Confirm your pilot knows how to file it — a hobbyist who wings it is a liability nightmare.
- **Match the sensor to the job.** Rooftop assessments after a hail event need RGB plus thermal; a cell tower structural survey needs high-resolution zoom optics; a solar array efficiency audit needs multispectral. Ask specifically what camera payload they're flying, not just what drone model.
- **Ask for a sample deliverable, not just a portfolio.** A folder of pretty aerial photos isn't the same as a geotagged inspection report with thermal overlays and measurement annotations. The report is what your insurer or structural engineer actually uses.
- **Check for relevant vertical experience.** Wilmington's industrial base — the Port, the chemical plants along the Christina River, the utility infrastructure running through New Castle County — presents different operational constraints than residential rooftop work. A pilot who's only done insurance claims isn't automatically the right fit for a tank farm inspection.
- **Confirm insurance.** $1M minimum hull and liability coverage is the floor for any commercial engagement. Get the certificate of insurance, not just their word.

> **Pro Tip:** Delaware has no state-level drone licensing on top of FAA Part 107, but if your project touches any state-owned infrastructure (bridges, DOT right-of-way, Port Authority facilities), check with the relevant agency — some require advance notice or a site-specific agreement regardless of airspace authorization.

## What to Expect

Commercial drone inspections in Wilmington run $500–3,500 depending on asset type, flight time, and deliverable complexity — a basic residential roof assessment is toward the low end, while a multi-structure industrial facility with thermal analysis and a formal engineering report lands toward the top. > **Reality Check:** The cheapest quote almost always means one of three things: no thermal capability, no report (just a photo dump), or no proper insurance. If someone's quoting you $200 for a commercial roof inspection, they're cutting a corner somewhere that will cost you more later — especially if the report ends up in litigation.
