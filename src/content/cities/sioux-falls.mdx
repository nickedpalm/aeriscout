## How to Choose a Drone Inspection Service in Sioux Falls

- **Verify the FAA Part 107 certificate before anything else.** Anyone charging money to fly commercially needs it. Ask for the certificate number and cross-check it at FAA's DroneZone. You'd be surprised how many "operators" skip this step and leave you holding the liability.
- **Match the sensor payload to your job.** A roofing contractor assessing hail damage after a South Dakota spring storm needs RGB and thermal — don't hire someone who only brings RGB. Thermal imaging catches moisture intrusion and insulation gaps that photos miss entirely.
- **Ask about insurance, specifically hull and liability.** A $1M general liability policy is the floor for any serious commercial operator. If they hesitate on this question, that tells you something.
- **Check for industry-specific certifications.** For cell tower or transmission line work near Sioux Falls's Xcel Energy infrastructure, AUVSI CUSP and ITC Level I thermography credentials signal real investment in the craft. For construction documentation, ask whether they've done progress reporting on projects of comparable scale.
- **Get the deliverable spec in writing.** "We'll send you photos" is not a deliverable spec. You want geotagged image sets, thermal overlays if applicable, and a written inspection report — with a specific turnaround window (24–48 hours is standard).

> **Pro Tip:** South Dakota has no state-level drone regulations beyond FAA rules, which is actually a plus for scheduling. But Sioux Falls sits near Sioux Falls Regional Airport (FSD) — confirm your operator knows how to pull LAANC authorizations for controlled airspace, because the approach corridor covers more of the city than most people realize.

## What to Expect

Pricing for commercial drone inspections in Sioux Falls runs $500–$3,500 per engagement depending on asset complexity, sensor requirements, and deliverable depth — a single residential roof assessment sits at the low end; a full transmission line survey or multi-building industrial inspection with thermal reporting hits the top. Most operators quote flat project rates, not hourly.

> **Reality Check:** The biggest pricing mistake clients make is hiring the cheapest bidder for a job that requires thermal imaging, then paying a second operator to redo it correctly. The $300 difference upfront becomes a $1,500 problem when the insurance adjuster rejects a claim because the imagery doesn't meet documentation standards.

Turnaround on standard deliverables is 24–48 hours for photo sets and basic reports; thermal analysis and annotated overlays may run 3–5 business days depending on the operator's post-processing workflow.
