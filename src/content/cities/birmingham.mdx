## How to Choose a Drone Inspection Service in Birmingham

- **Verify FAA Part 107 certification before anything else.** This isn't a nicety — it's federal law for commercial UAV operations. Ask for the Remote Pilot Certificate number and cross-check it at the FAA's DroneZone registry. Any operator who hesitates on this one is a liability.
- **Match the sensor to the job.** RGB cameras work for construction documentation and rooftop condition reports. Thermal imaging (ITC Level I or ASNT Level II certified pilots) is non-negotiable for solar array inspections, energy audits, and post-storm insurance assessments. A pilot running the wrong payload wastes your money.
- **Ask about Birmingham's Class B airspace coordination.** Birmingham-Shuttlesworth International Airport sits 5 miles northeast of downtown. Legitimate operators know the FAA LAANC authorization process cold — if your pilot acts surprised when you mention airspace, walk away.
- **Request geotagged deliverables, not just a PDF.** A professional inspection produces geotagged photo sets, thermal overlays, and measurement data. If they're handing you a flat photo album with no metadata, you're paying for a drone hobbyist's afternoon.
- **Check for industry certs beyond Part 107.** For industrial work — cell towers, transmission lines, bridge decks — look for AUVSI CUSP certification or OSHA 10-Hour Construction Safety. For post-storm insurance work after Alabama's severe weather season, FEMA IS-1505 UAS Disaster Operations training signals a pilot who's worked real catastrophe response.

> **Pro Tip:** Alabama sits squarely in Tornado Alley's eastern corridor. After any significant storm event, Birmingham-area operators get booked out within 48 hours by insurance adjusters. If you're managing a property portfolio, establish a relationship with two or three certified pilots *before* hail season — not after.

## What to Expect

Drone inspections in Birmingham run $500–$3,500 depending on asset type, required sensors, and report complexity — a residential rooftop assessment sits at the low end, while a full thermal scan of a commercial solar array or a multi-span bridge survey with measurement deliverables pushes toward the top. Most professional operators turn around reports within 24–48 hours; anything promised faster than that for complex thermal work should make you skeptical about analysis quality.

> **Reality Check:** The cheapest quote almost always reflects a consumer-grade drone and zero post-processing. The inspection fee is a fraction of what you'll spend on scaffolding, rope access, or — worse — missing a structural defect that a thermal overlay would have flagged. Don't optimize for the hourly rate on a $2M roof.
