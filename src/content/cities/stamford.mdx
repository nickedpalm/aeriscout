## How to Choose a Drone Inspection Service in Stamford

- **Verify FAA Part 107 certification — and then verify it again.** The FAA's Airmen Inquiry database is public. Anyone flying commercially in Connecticut without it is operating illegally, and your insurance won't cover an incident from their flight. Stamford sits just outside the [New York](/locations/new-york/new-york/) SFRA and adjacent to Westchester County Airport airspace — you want a pilot who's filed COAs before, not one learning on your job.
- **Match the sensor to the job.** RGB cameras are fine for construction documentation and basic roof surveys. Thermal imaging (ITC Level I or ASNT Level II certified operators) is non-negotiable for solar array inspections, HVAC assessments, or post-storm moisture infiltration work. Ask which sensor package comes standard and what's an upcharge.
- **Ask for a sample deliverable, not just a portfolio.** You want to see the actual report format — geotagged photo sets, thermal overlays, measurement annotations — not just pretty hero shots. A 24–48 hour turnaround is standard; anything longer signals a one-person shop with a backlog problem.
- **Check their insurance.** Commercial UAS operators in Connecticut should carry at minimum $1M in hull and liability coverage. Get the certificate of insurance before they fly, not after something goes sideways on a $2M rooftop.
- **For utility and cell tower work, look for AUVSI CUSP certification.** The FAA Part 107 is the floor, not the ceiling. Operators working transmission lines or vertical structures (Stamford has no shortage of high-rises and telecom infrastructure along I-95) should have documented experience with confined airspace and vertical asset inspection protocols.

> **Pro Tip:** If you're a general contractor pulling permits in Stamford, ask your drone operator if they've worked with the city's building department before. Familiarity with local permit timing and inspection windows will save you more headaches than any camera upgrade.

## What to Expect

Drone inspection jobs in Stamford run $500–$3,500 depending on asset type, scope, and deliverable complexity — a single-family roof assessment is on the low end, while a multi-structure industrial facility survey with thermal overlays and a full anomaly report sits at the top. Most operators charge travel fees for jobs outside their primary coverage zone, and same-day turnaround typically runs a 25–50% premium over the standard 24–48 hour window.

> **Reality Check:** The lowest quote is almost never the best value. Operators undercutting market rate by 40% are usually skipping liability insurance, flying consumer-grade hardware, or delivering JPEG dumps instead of structured inspection reports. An insurer or GC who accepts a $350 "inspection" and then can't use the output for a claim or permit just paid twice.
