## How to Choose a Drone Inspection Service in Little Rock

- **Verify FAA Part 107 certification before anything else.** A recreational pilot with a DJI Mavic and a YouTube tutorial is not the same as a licensed commercial UAV operator. Ask for their Remote Pilot Certificate number and check it at the FAA Airmen Inquiry database — takes 30 seconds and eliminates half the field.
- **Match the sensor to the job.** RGB cameras work for construction progress docs and roof condition assessments. Thermal imaging (look for ITC Level I or ASNT Level II credentials) is non-negotiable for solar array inspections, HVAC surveys, and moisture intrusion mapping. Don't pay for a thermal inspection from someone who only owns an RGB rig.
- **Check their Class B airspace authorization.** Little Rock sits under the Class C airspace of Bill and Hillary Clinton National Airport (LIT). Any legitimate commercial operator flying near downtown or the I-630 corridor needs LAANC authorization or a specific FAA waiver — if they can't explain how they handle airspace authorization, keep moving.
- **Ask for a sample deliverable, not just a portfolio.** Pretty photos don't tell you if the operator understands report structure. A professional deliverable includes geotagged imagery, a flight log, thermal overlays with anomaly callouts, and a written summary — not a Dropbox link with 300 unlabeled JPEGs.
- **Confirm liability insurance coverage.** Commercial drone operators should carry at minimum $1M hull and liability. Arkansas doesn't mandate it, but any utility, insurer, or general contractor worth working with will require a certificate of insurance before wheels-up.

> **Pro Tip:** For storm damage documentation in Pulaski County — especially after the severe hail seasons Central Arkansas sees in spring — ask specifically whether the operator has experience working alongside insurance adjusters. The workflow is different: chain of custody for imagery matters, and report format needs to match what carriers actually use for claims processing.

## What to Expect

A standard commercial drone inspection in Little Rock runs $500–$1,500 for residential and light commercial work (single roof, solar array up to 50 panels, basic progress documentation), with complex industrial jobs — transmission line surveys, cell tower inspections, large-scale thermal mapping — landing in the $2,000–$3,500 range depending on mobilization distance and deliverable complexity. Most operators turn around reports within 24–48 hours for standard jobs; same-day delivery is available for storm-surge situations but expect a premium.

> **Reality Check:** The cheapest quote is almost never the right quote for anything beyond a basic residential roof check. Low-ball bids typically mean consumer-grade equipment, no thermal capability, and a report that consists of photos with no metadata. For anything going into an insurance claim file or a structural engineering report, that's a liability — yours, not theirs.
