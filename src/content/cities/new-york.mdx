## How to Choose a Drone Inspection Service in New York

- **Verify Part 107 certification and check for NYC airspace waivers.** New York City sits under Class B airspace with LAANC authorization requirements, and dense urban flight corridors around bridges, towers, and high-rises often require additional coordination with the FAA. Operators who can't speak fluently about their LAANC approvals or waiver history are not ready for this market.

- **Ask for proof of liability insurance — specifically, the coverage amount.** Most commercial jobs in New York require a minimum of $1 million in liability coverage. For utility or infrastructure work, $2 million is standard. Don't accept "we're insured" — ask for the certificate of insurance.

- **Match the sensor package to your inspection type.** RGB cameras are fine for construction documentation and basic roof surveys, but thermal (ITC Level I or ASNT Level II certified) is non-negotiable for HVAC/roofing anomaly detection, solar panel surveys, and electrical infrastructure work. Confirm the pilot is certified to interpret the thermal data, not just capture it.

- **Check their urban flight experience, not just their drone model.** Flying in Manhattan around tall buildings, rotor wash reflection, GPS signal degradation, and heavy ATC traffic is a different skill set than open-field inspections. Ask for examples of comparable jobs in similar environments.

- **Request the deliverable format upfront.** Geotagged photo sets, thermal overlays, and measurement reports are standard — but confirm whether you're getting raw files, a PDF report, or a structured data package compatible with your asset management or insurance workflow. Turnaround time in New York is typically 24-48 hours.

> **Pro Tip:** For work on bridges, power infrastructure, or NYC DOT-adjacent assets, ask if the operator has experience coordinating with the relevant agency. Operators who've navigated MTA or Con Edison approval processes are worth their premium.

## What to Expect

Drone inspection pricing in New York runs $500–$3,500 per inspection depending on asset type, flight complexity, sensor requirements, and deliverable scope — a single-family roof survey sits at the low end, while a multi-tower utility survey or post-storm insurance documentation package lands at the top. Most operators charge a half-day or full-day rate for larger commercial sites rather than per-asset.

> **Reality Check:** If you're getting quotes at the low end for complex urban work, ask exactly what's included. Underbid operators often exclude data processing, report generation, or thermal analysis from the base quote — and those line items can double the final invoice. Get scope-inclusive pricing in writing.

## Local Market Overview

New York City's combination of aging infrastructure, dense high-rise construction activity, and one of the highest concentrations of insurance carriers and adjusters in the country makes it one of the strongest markets in the US for drone inspection services — and one of the most regulated. Operators working in the five boroughs need to be conversant not just in FAA Part 107, but in NYC's own drone regulations under Local Law 61 and the city's ongoing coordination requirements for work near critical infrastructure. If an operator doesn't know what Local Law 61 requires, that's your cue to keep looking.
