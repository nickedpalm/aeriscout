## How to Choose a Drone Inspection Service in Tampa

- **Verify FAA Part 107 certification, not just "FAA licensed."** Ask for the certificate number and cross-check it at [faa.gov/uas/recreational_flyers/knowledge_test_updates](https://faa.gov/). Florida has no additional state licensing layer, so federal Part 107 is the floor — not a differentiator.
- **Check their insurance, not just that they have it.** You want a minimum of $1M general liability with a specific UAV endorsement. Many Tampa pilots carry auto policies that explicitly exclude commercial drone operations. Ask for the certificate of insurance naming your company as additionally insured.
- **Match the sensor to the job.** RGB cameras work for construction progress documentation. Thermal (FLIR-grade) is non-negotiable for roof moisture surveys, solar array inspections, and electrical equipment. If you're hiring for hurricane damage documentation — common in Tampa Bay after storm season — confirm they have both, and that the pilot holds an ITC Level I or ASNT Level II thermography certification, not just a camera with a thermal lens.
- **Ask about their Hillsborough/Pinellas airspace experience.** Tampa Executive (VDF), Tampa International (TPA), and MacDill AFB create overlapping Class B, C, and restricted airspace that blankets much of the metro. A pilot who doesn't reflexively mention LAANC authorization workflows when you ask about a downtown inspection is a pilot who's going to delay your project.
- **Request sample deliverables before you commit.** A professional inspection package means geotagged photo sets, a PDF report with annotated findings, and thermal overlays if applicable — delivered within 24-48 hours. If their "deliverable" is a Google Drive link with 400 unlabeled JPEGs, that's your answer.

> **Pro Tip:** For post-storm insurance claims in Tampa Bay, turnaround speed is everything — adjusters are processing hundreds of claims simultaneously. Ask specifically whether the pilot has experience with rapid loss documentation workflows and whether they can deliver preliminary findings within 24 hours of flight.

## What to Expect

Most drone inspections in Tampa run $500–$1,500 for residential and light commercial work (single roof, solar array, small cell tower), with complex industrial or multi-structure jobs reaching $2,500–$3,500 once you factor in thermography analysis, detailed reporting, and multiple flight sessions. The inspection itself typically takes 1–3 hours on site; the full deliverable package — geotagged imagery, annotated report, thermal overlays — lands within 24–48 hours in most cases.

> **Reality Check:** Don't anchor on the hourly rate. A $150/hour pilot who delivers 400 raw photos and a two-line email is more expensive than a $300/hour pilot who gives you a 20-page annotated report your insurance adjuster can actually use. The report format is where cheap pilots quietly cost you money downstream.
