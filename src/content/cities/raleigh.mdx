## How to Choose a Drone Inspection Service in Raleigh

- **Verify the FAA Part 107 certificate, not just the claim.** Any pilot operating commercially in North Carolina is required by federal law to hold a Remote Pilot Certificate. Ask for the certificate number and cross-reference it at the FAA DroneZone registry. If they hedge, move on.
- **Match the sensor package to your job type.** RGB cameras handle rooftop documentation and construction progress fine. If you're doing a solar array inspection for a Research Triangle energy project or assessing storm damage for a thermal anomaly claim, you need a pilot carrying a calibrated thermal camera — and ideally an ITC Level I or ASNT Level II thermography certification to interpret what they shoot.
- **Check airspace restrictions before you schedule.** Raleigh-Durham International sits roughly 10 miles northwest of downtown, which puts large chunks of the metro inside Class C airspace. Legitimate pilots will pull a LAANC authorization before flying near RDU — if they don't mention airspace at all during the quote call, that's a red flag.
- **Ask for a sample deliverable, not just a portfolio.** Any operator can show you a pretty aerial photo. What you actually need is a geotagged photo set, a thermal overlay (if applicable), and measurement data — formatted in a way your GC, insurer, or structural engineer can actually use. Ask to see a real report from a comparable job.
- **Confirm insurance and a certificate of insurance.** North Carolina doesn't mandate commercial UAS insurance at the state level, but your general liability exposure is real. Require a COI showing at least $1M hull and liability coverage before any pilot takes off on your property.

> **Pro Tip:** For post-storm insurance documentation in the Triangle — hail season runs hard through April and May here — turnaround speed is everything. Ask specifically whether the operator can deliver a preliminary thermal + RGB report within 24 hours. Operators who batch-process at the end of the week will cost you on claim timelines.

## What to Expect

A standard commercial drone inspection in Raleigh runs $500–$3,500 depending on asset type, sensor package, and report complexity — a residential roof assessment lands toward the low end, while a full transmission line survey or multi-structure industrial inspection pushes toward the top. Most certified operators deliver geotagged photo sets and a written findings report within 24–48 hours of the flight date.

> **Reality Check:** The cheapest quote usually reflects a stripped deliverable — raw photos with no thermal overlay, no measurement data, and no written findings. That's fine for simple documentation, but it will not satisfy an insurance adjuster or a structural engineer. Know what you're buying before you compare quotes on price alone.
