## How to Choose a Drone Inspection Service in Virginia Beach

- **Verify Part 107 and check their airspace experience.** Virginia Beach sits adjacent to some of the most complex restricted airspace in the country — NAS Oceana, Dam Neck Annex, and active military corridors running over the coast. Your pilot needs documented experience filing waivers and coordinating with Virginia Beach ATC, not just a certificate they printed six months ago.
- **Match the sensor to the job.** RGB cameras handle construction documentation and roof assessments. Thermal (ITC Level I or ASNT Level II certified operators) is what you need for solar array inspections, post-storm moisture mapping, or electrical infrastructure surveys. Don't pay for a thermal flight if your pilot isn't certified to read the data.
- **Ask for a sample deliverable before you book.** A serious operator hands you a portfolio: geotagged photo sets, annotated thermal overlays, measurement outputs. If their "report" is a Dropbox folder of JPEGs, keep looking.
- **Check coastal weather protocols.** Virginia Beach's Atlantic exposure means wind and salt spray are constants. Ask how the operator handles postponements, what wind thresholds ground their fleet, and whether equipment is rated for marine environments.
- **Confirm insurance minimums.** For commercial work in Virginia Beach — especially anything near the resort strip or over active construction — you want operators carrying at least $1M in hull and liability coverage. Anything less is a risk you're absorbing.

> **Pro Tip:** For post-hurricane roof documentation (a real use case here — Virginia Beach takes direct hits), FEMA IS-1505 UAS Disaster Operations certification matters. It means the pilot has trained specifically for post-disaster deployment conditions, not just fair-weather residential inspections.

## What to Expect

A standard commercial drone inspection in Virginia Beach runs $500–$1,500 for residential roofing and smaller structures; complex utility, tower, or industrial work lands in the $1,500–$3,500 range depending on flight time, sensor package, and report depth. Most operators deliver geotagged imagery within 24 hours and full written reports within 48. 

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake is hiring on day rate alone. A $400 inspection that comes back as a folder of unprocessed images with no annotations, no measurements, and no thermal analysis isn't cheap — it's worthless. Clarify deliverables in writing before the flight.
